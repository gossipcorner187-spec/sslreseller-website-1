Post-Quantum Cryptography

Post-quantum cryptography refers to cryptographic algorithms that are designed to be secure against attacks from quantum computers. Unlike traditional encryption methods like RSA and ECC, which could be broken by quantum algorithms such as Shor’s algorithm, post-quantum systems rely on mathematical problems that remain hard even for quantum machines.

This is becoming increasingly important as future quantum computers may threaten current SSL/TLS security systems.
